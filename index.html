Understand & Minimise Risk

Here we explain how the new regulations expose agencies to increased risk.

Why Rationalise Your PSL?

A good PSL is more than a list of companies you're prepared to work with, it's the assurance regime that protects your business, clients, and contractors.

A strong PSL includes only thoroughly vetted partners who demonstrate proven compliance, reliability, and service excellence. To protect your business, each supplier should undergo robust due diligence and regular audits.

Key Point: The April 2026 reforms make agencies jointly liable for unpaid tax and NICs if umbrella companies fail to comply. This significantly increases the financial and reputational risk of working with non-compliant providers.

The Risks of Poor PSL Management

  • • Financial liability for unpaid taxes and NICs
  • • Reputational damage from association with non-compliant providers
  • • Legal and regulatory penalties
  • • Loss of client trust and business relationships
  • • Operational disruption from provider failures

Benefits of a Well-Managed PSL

  • Protection from financial and legal liabilities
  • Enhanced reputation and market credibility
  • Improved service quality and reliability
  • Stronger relationships with compliant partners
  • Peace of mind for your business and clients

PSL Compliance Framework

A comprehensive framework for evaluating and maintaining compliant suppliers.

2.1 Company Structure & Legitimacy

Before engaging any umbrella company, it's vital to understand exactly who you are contracting with and who is legally responsible for employing workers.

Understanding the corporate structure is important. Make sure you know which legal entity you are engaging with, and which entity is ultimately responsible for employing the workers being supplied.

You should take the time to verify company registration with Companies House (active status, directors and registered address) as well as recent filings and confirmation statements.

We would also recommend that you verify VAT registration and confirm PAYE registration with HMRC. These checks are very simple to carry out.

Reputable umbrella companies will readily provide formal documents that prove they operate legally and transparently.

Entities that delay or refuse to share documentation may be hiding non-compliant structures.

Ask for:

  • A copy of the organisational chart
  • Location of the company engaging the workers
  • Details and credibility of ownership
  • Details of key stakeholders, legal and compliance teams, day-to-day support

And verify:

  • Company registration with Companies House
  • Check recent filings and confirmation statements
  • Verify VAT registration with HMRC
  • Confirm PAYE registration with HMRC

2.3 Contracts & Payments

Clear, compliant contracts are the foundation of any legitimate umbrella arrangement. Review all contract templates to ensure they accurately reflect the employment relationship and comply with current legislation.

Payment terms must be transparent and fair. Workers should receive timely payment with all statutory deductions correctly calculated and clearly itemized on payslips.

Ensure contracts clearly state employment status and that workers understand their rights and obligations.

Ask for:

  • Sample contract templates
  • Example payslips showing deductions
  • Payment terms and schedules
  • Expense reimbursement policies

And verify:

  • Contracts comply with employment law
  • Payment terms are fair and transparent
  • Statutory deductions are correct
  • Timely payment to workers

2.4 Operational & Payroll Controls

Expenses

Expense policies must comply with HMRC guidelines. Ensure proper documentation and approval processes are in place, with no disguised remuneration schemes.

Holiday

Verify compliance with Working Time Regulations and accurate calculation of holiday entitlement. Check proper accrual and payment processes.

Right to Work

Robust right to work checking processes are essential. Verify documentation storage, security, and compliance with immigration legislation.

Pensions

Confirm auto-enrolment compliance, pension scheme registration, and proper contribution calculations and payments.

Key Controls to Verify:

  • Expense policies comply with HMRC
  • Holiday calculations are accurate
  • Right to work checks are robust
  • VAT registration and compliance
  • Agency Workers Regulations adherence
  • Auto-enrolment pension compliance
  • Proper documentation processes

2.5 Asking the Right Questions

When evaluating potential PSL partners, it's essential to ask probing questions that reveal their true compliance status and operational capabilities.

Red Flags: Be wary of providers who cannot provide clear answers, lack proper documentation, offer unusually low fees, or promote tax-efficient schemes. These may indicate compliance risks.

Key Questions to Ask

  • • How do you ensure compliance with current tax and employment legislation?
  • • What audits or certifications do you hold?
  • • Can you provide evidence of your insurance coverage?
  • • How do you handle HMRC enquiries or investigations?
  • • What is your process for staying updated on regulatory changes?

Due Diligence Checklist:

  • Review company accounts and financial statements
  • Verify insurance certificates and coverage
  • Check Companies House records
  • Review sample contracts and documentation
  • Request HMRC compliance evidence
  • Obtain client references
  • Review audit reports and certifications
  • Assess technology and data security
  • Verify professional memberships

3 Documentation Requirements

Maintaining comprehensive documentation is crucial for demonstrating due diligence and protecting your business in the event of HMRC enquiries or audits.

Company Information

Collect certificates of incorporation, latest financial statements, company structure charts, credit reports, and all business licenses and registrations.

Insurance & Compliance

Verify all insurance certificates including Employers' Liability, Professional Indemnity, and Public Liability. Request audit reports and HMRC compliance confirmations.

Operational Documentation

Review sample contracts, worker handbooks, payslip examples, expense policies, holiday policies, and pension scheme documentation.

Best Practice: Maintain a central repository for all PSL documentation with version control and regular review cycles. Ensure documents are updated annually or when significant changes occur.

Essential Documents:

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Financial statements and accounts
  • Insurance certificates (all types)
  • Audit reports and certifications
  • Sample employment contracts
  • Worker handbooks and policies
  • Payslip examples
  • HMRC compliance confirmations
  • Reference checks and testimonials
  • Annual review documentation
